# ORRO Multi-chain Pivot

Implementing **Alpha Phase 2: The Multi-Chain Pivot** is a major strategic move that acknowledges the reality of the market. Ethereum is the home of "Legacy Trust" and deep liquidity, while Solana is the "High-Performance" engine for micro-transactions and high-frequency grit.

By allowing users to choose their chain, you are letting them decide between **Security/Longevity (ETH)** and **Speed/Accessibility (SOL)**.

## Phase 2 Technical Roadmap: "The Dual-Anchor"

To make this seamless for the "Guides" without splitting the community, we will implement the following:

### 1. Chain-Agnostic User Identity

The **ORRO Profile** (and the 1:1 Trust Score) will act as a "Meta-Layer."

* **Unified ID:** A user's reputation is stored in a decentralized "Identity Vault."

* **Cross-Chain Sync:** Whether they mint a fragment on ETH or SOL, their **Global Trust Score** updates in real-time. A point earned on Solana is worth the same as a point earned on Ethereum.

### 2. Market-Driven Choice

In the **Advanced Minting** menu, the user will see a "Chain Selection" toggle:

* **Solana (Default):** For daily high-frequency fragments. Near-zero cost ($<0.01).

* **Ethereum (Premium):** For high-value project completion or "Final Master" assets where long-term censorship resistance is worth the higher gas fee ($5-$50+).

### 3. Interoperability via "The Bridge"

To avoid "siloed" projects, we will use a protocol like **Wormhole** or **LayerZero**:

* **Asset Mobility:** A creator can start a project on Solana during the "BETA Daily Grind" and, once they reach **Level 4 (Creator)**, bridge their project to Ethereum for final certification and sale.

* **Unified Gossip Box:** The Live Ticker will aggregate messages from both chains into one global stream, keeping the community together.

## Comparison for User Decision-Making

| **Feature**         | **Solana (Alpha Phase 1)**           | **Ethereum (Alpha Phase 2)**      |

## Operational Impact on "The Golden Ratio"

The 1:1 ratio remains the same. The protocol doesn't care _where_ you prove your grit, only that you _did_ it.
